My name is Daniel. If you want to know a bit about me you are in the right place. If not, here is a convenient door to kittens, thanks for stopping by!

I'm a queer mentally ill amateur coder, a sworn enemy of stigma and social prejudices, a strong believer of equity.
I like to code, read, play video games, and listen to music.

I was born in Canary Islands sometime around 1990. Later I crossed the ocean to study Computer Science and Mathematics in Madrid, and eventually did an exchange year at EPFL, Switzerland. This inevitably led to a Master's Degree in Computer Science at said university, during which I had fun working on programming languages and big data. I moved to Zurich for my dream job in 2016.

Then came the Plot Twist: I was diagnosed with Bipolar II Disorder. It got so bad that I had to stop working in 2017, and I haven't been able to go back ever since. My illness is very resistant to treatment, and it has been a long and painful journey. I'm very open an unashamed about it, and you can read more about it in my blog.

I identify as agender. I feel some connection to gender, but it is faint and not a big part of my life or identity. If gender was ice cream, I would be vanilla. On the internet I use they/them. If you want to know more about my journey into queerness, you can read about it in my blog.
